Hamilton is a chess-playing AI written in CPP. However, it does not play conventional chess, rather a modified version created by the instructor called Congress Chess. The full report of the game can be viewed here: https://athena.ecs.csus.edu/~gordonvs/180/Tournaments/19Stres.html.

Hamilton placed second among the students in the class and even going 1-1 with the professor's program, Progress.

Features include
	- Minimax Search
	- Alpha/Beta Pruning
	- History Heuristic
	- Killer Move Heuristic
	- Move ordering (based on capture, killer move, most valuable capture)
	- bit boards

Hamilton3 is the most stable with Hamilton(1) trying to (ineffectively) utilize transition tables.
Hamilton3 should be used.
